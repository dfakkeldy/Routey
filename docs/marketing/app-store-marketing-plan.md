# Marketing Strategy: Routey

## App Profile

| Attribute | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Category | Productivity |
| Model | Pricing not final. Working assumption: paid upfront or one-time unlock; no subscriptions currently exist. |
| Stage | Pre-launch / internal TestFlight |
| Audience | Rural mail carriers who need offline route lookup, parcel sorting, and delivery-day memory. |
| Platform | iPhone first. watchOS and CarPlay are planned after V1.0. |
| Launch target | Kickstart currently shows 33 days until launch: August 3, 2026. |

## Strategic Goals

1. Make the App Store page immediately understandable to a carrier in the depot:
   offline route memory, Snap-to-Add, Today's Run.
2. Prove trust before asking for money: private iCloud, no accounts, no ads, no
   analytics, no carrier affiliation, invented public examples only.
3. Build a small but credible launch channel through TestFlight users,
   build-in-public updates, and carrier-focused word of mouth.

## Current Constraints

- Competitor positioning work is not done. Kickstart lists competitor apps,
  positioning, reviews, pricing, onboarding, paywalls, screenshots, and App
  Store copy as due or overdue.
- Pricing is not final. Kickstart still tracks "Set pricing tiers" and "Define
  free vs paid boundary" for July 7, 2026.
- No App Store traffic exists yet, so Product Page Optimization tests and Custom
  Product Pages would not produce reliable data.
- Routey is public and carrier-agnostic. Marketing assets must not include real
  route data, real street/site/place names, civic numbers, employer names, or
  carrier-specific jargon.

## Recommended Features Eligible Now

### High Priority

#### App Store Assets and Screenshot Storyboard

- **Why**: Routey needs visual proof of the unusual workflow: import/search,
  Snap-to-Add, Today's Run, offline confidence, and privacy.
- **Effort**: Moderate.
- **Impact**: High confidence for first downloads and review.
- **Action**: Build a 6-8 screenshot sequence using invented fixture data.
  Avoid watchOS and CarPlay screenshots until those surfaces exist.

#### Default Product Page Copy and ASO

- **Why**: A niche paid/pro tool needs a precise first sentence and search terms
  before launch traffic arrives.
- **Effort**: Quick win.
- **Impact**: Better conversion from the small audience that does find it.
- **Action**: Refine title/subtitle/keywords around offline route, parcel
  sorting, route lookup, OCR, rural delivery, and checklist workflows. Do not
  mention carrier systems or competing platforms.

#### Build-in-Public Devlog Cadence

- **Why**: Routey has a narrow audience and a credible creator story. Weekly
  public progress can turn trust into testers.
- **Effort**: Quick win now that devlog automation exists.
- **Impact**: Small but compounding awareness.
- **Action**: Keep weekly devlog PRs flowing and cross-post human-edited
  summaries only after removing route-specific details.

### Medium Priority

#### App Store Featuring Nomination

- **Why**: Routey has a strong productivity/privacy/offline angle, but featuring
  should wait until the V1.0 phone workflow is demonstrable.
- **Effort**: Moderate.
- **Impact**: Potentially high, uncertain.
- **Action**: Draft a nomination after the device smoke pass. Pitch offline
  rural productivity, privacy-first personal route data, and on-device OCR.

#### Pre-Order Readiness

- **Why**: Pre-orders can collect intent before launch if the App Store page and
  screenshots are polished.
- **Effort**: Moderate.
- **Impact**: Medium if there is enough off-store launch traffic.
- **Action**: Revisit after pricing is decided and the final screenshot set is
  ready. Do not enable pre-order with placeholder metadata.

#### Reviewer and Tester Outreach

- **Why**: A niche utility benefits from small, qualified feedback more than
  broad paid acquisition at this stage.
- **Effort**: Moderate.
- **Impact**: High product-signal quality.
- **Action**: Invite a small set of carriers/testers with clear instructions,
  invented sample-data guidance, and focused feedback prompts.

### Nice to Have Later

#### In-App Event for Launch

- **Why**: A launch event can signal "new workflow for parcel-heavy mornings,"
  but only once the App Store listing is complete.
- **Effort**: Moderate.
- **Impact**: Low to medium pre-traction.
- **Action**: Plan a launch event, but prioritize screenshots, metadata, and the
  device smoke pass first.

#### Paid Acquisition

- **Why**: Apple Search Ads can find niche keywords, but the product page needs
  enough polish and a clear price before spending.
- **Effort**: Moderate and ongoing.
- **Impact**: Unknown until conversion baseline exists.
- **Action**: Do not spend until launch metadata, pricing, and screenshots are
  final. Start with tiny exact-match experiments only.

## Not Yet Recommended

| Feature | Minimum Needed | Current Level | When to Revisit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product Page Optimization | About 1,000 impressions/week | No live App Store traffic | After launch analytics show enough impressions. |
| Custom Product Pages | About 5,000 impressions/month or distinct campaigns | No campaigns and one primary audience | After paid/search/social channels split into distinct segments. |
| Promoted IAP | A configured IAP product | No IAP model finalized | If Routey becomes freemium or one-time unlock. |
| Win-back/promotional offers | Subscription/IAP audience and churn signal | No paying user base | Not relevant until a monetization model and user base exist. |
| Watch/CarPlay creative | Shipping watchOS/CarPlay app surfaces | Deferred | After those targets are implemented and reviewed. |

   traffic exists.

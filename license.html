In a nutshell Routey is licensed under the GNU General Public License, version 3 or later. A special App Store Distribution Exception lets it be shipped through Apple's App Store without giving up its open-source guarantees — as long as the full source stays public under the GPL.

What the GPL-3.0 gives you

As a user and a developer, the GPL guarantees four freedoms:

  • Use Routey for any purpose.
  • Study how it works — the complete source code is public.
  • Share copies with anyone.
  • Modify it and share your changes.

The GPL is a copyleft license, which adds one important condition: if you distribute Routey or a modified version of it, you must do so under the GPL too, and make the corresponding source available. In other words, the software stays free for everyone downstream — nobody can take it closed-source.

Why there's an App Store Exception

There's a well-known tension between the GPL and app stores. Apple's App Store imposes terms the GPL doesn't allow on their own — you must accept Apple's Terms of Service, there are limits on the number of devices, and apps are wrapped in Apple's mandatory cryptographic signing / DRM. Strictly applied, those terms conflict with the GPL.

To resolve that, the copyright holder grants an additional permission under section 7 of the GPL — the App Store Distribution Exception. It explicitly allows Routey to be distributed through Apple's App Store (or a comparable platform), despite those platform terms.

This does not make Routey any less free. The exception applies only if:

  1. The complete corresponding source of the distributed version is also available to everyone under the GPL, through a channel without those restrictions — namely the public repository at github.com/dfakkeldy/Routey; and
  2. You are the copyright holder of the code you distribute, or have the copyright holder's permission to grant the exception for it.

What this means in practice

  • Carriers: use the app freely. If it's offered on the App Store, that's fully above-board — and the source is always here for you to inspect.
  • Tinkerers: clone the repo, build it, change it, run it on your own devices. No restrictions for personal use.
  • Redistributors: you may share or fork Routey, including to an app store, provided your version stays under the GPL and its source stays public.
A note on the name The "Routey" name and logo identify this project. The code is yours to fork under the GPL, but please ship a substantially modified fork under your own name so users aren't confused about which version they're running.

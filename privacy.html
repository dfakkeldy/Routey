Privacy Policy

Routey is built around a simple idea: your route is yours. Addresses, dog warnings, and customer flags are sensitive, so the app is designed to keep them with you.

Last updated 1 July 2026 · Describes the current pre-release build and intended V1.0 privacy model · This policy will be finalized and version-stamped when the app is released.

The short version Routey has no servers and no accounts. Your route lives on your device and backs up to your own private iCloud. There are no ads, no analytics, and no third-party trackers. The only way your data ever leaves is a file you choose to encrypt and hand to a relief carrier. In the current pre-release build, camera OCR is active; proof photos, delivery location capture, and encrypted file UI remain V1.0 release gates.

1. Who this applies to

This policy covers the Routey iOS app and its planned watchOS and CarPlay companions, and this website at dfakkeldy.github.io/Routey. Routey is an independent project created by Dan Fakkeldy. It is not affiliated with any postal or delivery service, or with Apple.

2. What Routey stores

To do its job, Routey keeps the information you put into it:

  • Your master route — stops, delivery points, community-mailbox sites, addresses, occupant names, tie-out codes, nicknames, and tags such as dog or no-flyers.
  • Today's Run and delivery records — what you delivered, the outcome, a timestamp, GPS coordinates, and an optional photo you take as proof of delivery.
  • Notes you add to stops, slots, points, or addresses.
  • Parcel information read from labels you scan, including any signature or customs keywords.

Routey does not ask for your name, email, phone number, or any account credentials. There is nothing to sign up for.

3. Where your data is stored

Your phone is the source of truth. Routey is offline-first: everything is stored in a local database on your device, and every feature works with no signal at all.

For backup and to keep your own devices in step, Routey syncs through your private iCloud database — the same personal iCloud your other apps use, tied to your Apple Account. This data is stored in your private iCloud space, governed by Apple's privacy policy. The Routey project operates no server and never receives a copy of your data.

No cloud sharing Routey does not use iCloud sharing or any public/shared database. Sync is to your private space only.

4. What leaves your device — and what doesn't

Only two things move your route data off the phone, and you control both:

  • Private iCloud sync (above) — automatic backup to your own iCloud, which you can turn off in iOS Settings.
  • An encrypted .routey file that you deliberately export to hand a route to a relief carrier (see §7).

Nothing else is transmitted. Routey contains no advertising networks, no analytics or crash-reporting SDKs, and no third-party trackers of any kind.

5. Camera & photos

The camera is used to read parcel labels and sort-case grids. Optical character recognition runs entirely on your device using Apple's on-device Vision framework — label images are processed locally and are not uploaded anywhere.

Proof-of-delivery photos you choose to take are stored as files on your device (and backed up via your private iCloud, like the rest of your data). Routey only accesses the camera and photos when you use a feature that needs them, after iOS asks your permission.

6. Location

Routey uses location for two purposes, both tied to delivering the route:

  • Proof of delivery — when you log a delivery, Routey records the GPS coordinates and a timestamp so you have a record of where and when.
  • Navigation V1.2 · CarPlay — for turn-by-turn directions, each stop's coordinates are geocoded once when you build the route, then reused. Routey does not continuously broadcast your live location to anyone.

Location data is stored with your route data on your device and your private iCloud — nowhere else.

7. The encrypted relief-handoff file

When you hand your route to someone covering for you, Routey exports it as a single .routey file. That file is encrypted with a passphrase you choose, using strong, industry-standard encryption (a PBKDF2 password-derived key and authenticated AES-256-GCM encryption). Practically, that means:

  • The file is unreadable without the passphrase — an incorrect passphrase simply fails to open it.
  • You send it however you like (AirDrop, Files, email); the Routey project is never involved and never sees it.
  • The recipient imports it read-only ("borrowed"), so your master route stays separate.
Your responsibility Choose a strong passphrase and share it through a separate channel from the file itself. Anyone with both the file and the passphrase can read the route inside it.

8. Children

Routey is a professional tool for mail carriers and is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age — there are no accounts and no data collection by the project at all.

9. This website

This site is served as static pages from GitHub Pages. It sets no tracking cookies and runs no analytics. It loads a web font from Google Fonts; your browser contacts Google's font servers to fetch it, subject to Google's privacy policy. Hosting is provided by GitHub, subject to GitHub's privacy statement.

10. Open source — verify it yourself

You don't have to take our word for any of this. Routey is free and open-source software under the GNU General Public License v3. The complete source code is public, so anyone can read exactly what the app does with your data. See the source repository and the license page.

11. Changes to this policy

Routey is in active development toward V1.0, so this policy may change as features are finished. Any material change will be reflected here with an updated date, and the final policy will be in place when the app is released.

12. Contact

Questions about privacy? Open an issue on the project's GitHub and we'll respond there.

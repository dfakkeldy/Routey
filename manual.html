01

Getting started

V1.0

Routey is built around your real master route — the sequence of stops you actually drive, with every box, compartment, and quirk. The first thing you'll do is get that route into the app. You have three ways, and you can mix them:

  • Import a CSV — export your stops from Reminders (or a spreadsheet) and Routey builds the stop sequence and addresses from it.
  • Photograph your sort case — Routey reads the grid of slots to bootstrap your Virtual Sort Case and help lay out community-mailbox sites.
  • Build it by hand — add and edit stops directly. The route is always editable, because a real route never stops changing.
Good to know The official tie-out sheet is a helpful reference, but Routey doesn't need it. Your route in Routey becomes the living source of truth — and it can regenerate a fresh tie-out sheet whenever you want one.
Step-by-step & screenshots coming soon. The exact import screens and the first-run setup flow are still being built. This section will get a guided walkthrough with pictures as V1.0 takes shape.
02

Building your master route

V1.0

Your master route is made of stops in drive order. Each stop is a place you physically pull over, and it can be one of four shapes — Routey handles all of them with one clean model:

  • A single roadside box — one customer.
  • A shared box — one receptacle, several customers.
  • A cluster of roadside boxes — several boxes at one pull-over, with the customers' doors often located elsewhere.
  • A community mailbox — a site with modules and compartments, parcel lockers, and the occasional CLOSED insert.

Stops carry their tie-out code (like 33 or 20), an optional nickname you actually use ("Cornerstore", "The Manor"), and any notes. Addresses are kept separate from the box that serves them, so a seniors' complex where everyone shares one civic number is disambiguated by occupant name — not crammed into one line.

Why it's built this way Separating the receptacle (the box) from the address (the customer and their door) is what lets Routey capture shared boxes, clustered RMBs, and community mailbox compartments without special cases. See the data model on the home page.
Editing screens in progress. Detailed walkthroughs for adding a community mailbox site, splitting a shared box, and reordering stops will appear here with screenshots.
03

The Virtual Sort Case

V1.0

The Virtual Sort Case is an on-screen mirror of your physical case — the same slots, in the same order, labelled by civic number and tie-out. Each slot maps to a delivery point, so a shared slot shows all the civic numbers it serves at a glance.

  • Add colour flags and notes to any slot — the digital replacement for the sticky notes stuck to your case.
  • Search jumps straight to a slot; shared slots disambiguate by showing every address.
  • Worn case strips? Routey can print fresh ones — see Reports.
Layout & flag UI being finalized. How slots are arranged on screen and how you attach flags is still settling. Walkthrough and screenshots to come.
04

Morning search — "is it on my route?"

V1.0

As you sort, you'll constantly want two answers: is this on my route? and where does it go? Type a civic number and Routey answers instantly — no spinners, filtering as you type.

A result shows you, at a glance:

  • The stop nickname or site, and the module / compartment / box it belongs to.
  • The door location, if it differs from the box.
  • Any tags — dog, no-flyers, signature, and so on.
Speed by design Search is powered by an on-device full-text index, so it keeps up with the pace you sort at — even with no signal.
05

Snapping parcels (OCR)

V1.0

This is the one nobody else does. Point your camera at a parcel label and Routey reads it on-device — address, recipient name, and keywords like SIGNATURE or customs — then matches it to your route and drops the parcel onto the right address in delivery order.

What happens after a snap depends on how confident the match is:

  • Confident match → the parcel is added automatically, with a quick undo toast in case it's wrong.
  • A few candidates → Routey shows a short list (with the raw label text and the differing field highlighted) so you tap the right one.
  • Low confidence → it falls back to predictive search so you can find it by hand in a second.

A barcode on the same label is read as a cross-check. Signature and customs parcels are flagged and tallied into a running "Today: N signatures" count, so you know what's coming before you even organize the truck.

Why the number matters A confidently-read civic number is decisive — "12 Main" will never be matched to "21 Main". When a number is shared across many units, the recipient's name on the label breaks the tie.
Capture screen & confirm flow in progress. The live camera UI, the disambiguation list, and the signature counter will be shown here with images as they're finished.
06

Planning Today's Run

V1.0

Each day Routey generates Today's Run — a working copy of your route for that date, with its own check-off state. It's yours to shape before you pull out:

  • Reorder by dragging — for construction, weather, or to fit a door visit in "when nearest". Optionally promote a change to your master route if it's permanent.
  • Filter the view — full route, no-flyers + parcels, just today's parcels, or just signatures.
  • Roll-ups at cluster and community mailbox stops show parcels per module and flyers per module, so you load the truck right.
One day, one device Today's Run is a single-device working instance — it avoids the merge hazards of two phones reordering the same day. Your master route still syncs across all your devices normally.
07

Delivering & logging

V1.0

On route, your next stop is front and centre. At a cluster or community mailbox you expand to the delivery points and handle mail and parcels — box versus door. Logging an outcome is one tap, and it records GPS, a timestamp, and an optional photo:

  • Delivered
  • Safedrop
  • Mailbox
  • In person
  • Not home — carded
  • Left at door
  • Next day

Finished a stretch all at once? Tap the last stop to bulk check-off every stop before it — handy when you've cleared a whole road.

Works with no signal Photos and outcomes are stored locally the instant you tap — they don't hang waiting for a tower, and they don't vanish when you drive off.
08

Follow-ups & warnings

V1.0

Some things can't be finished at the door. If a signature parcel needs a notice card dropped in a compartment, logging the failed attempt spawns a follow-up task that surfaces at the right community mailbox stop later in your run — so you don't forget to slip the card in M2-C7.

Warning tags like dog and scary-dog surface automatically — when that stop comes up, and when you scan a parcel for that address. No more learning about the dog the hard way.

09

History & Delivery Intelligence

V1.0

At the end of the day, Today's Run is archived into History, and a fresh run generates for tomorrow. History is fully searchable:

  • Find any past delivery by address, date, tag, or photo.
  • One-tap filters for flags and dog warnings.
  • Answer "did I deliver it, and when?" in seconds.
Turns the past into a heads-up Because history is indexed, scanning a label can warn you about an address before you get there — the route teaches you as you go.

Reports & printing

V1.0

Routey can regenerate the paper artifacts the office never keeps current. Each is a formatted PDF you can save, share, or send straight to AirPrint:

  • Tie-out sheet — your whole route in delivery order.
  • Case strips — printable slot labels to replace worn ones.
  • Filtered lists — e.g. every no-flyers stop, or all parcels delivered on a given date.

11

Relief handoff (encrypted .routey)

V1.0

When someone covers your route, they shouldn't have to relearn every quirk. Routey exports your whole route — stops, slots, tags, dog warnings, notes — as a single encrypted .routey file.

  1. Choose Export route and set a passphrase.
  2. Send the file however you like — AirDrop, Files, or email.
  3. The relief carrier opens it in Routey and enters the passphrase.
  4. It imports read-only ("borrowed") so your master stays separate.
Encrypted, not shared The file is locked with strong encryption tied to your passphrase. A wrong passphrase simply fails to open — there's no copy on any server, and nothing is shared through the cloud. This file is the only way a route leaves your device.
Export/import screens in progress. The passphrase prompt and the borrowed-route view will be shown here with screenshots.
12

Sync, backup & privacy

V1.0

Your phone holds the real copy of your route — Routey is offline-first, so every feature works in a dead zone and nothing waits on a tower. When you're back in range, your own private iCloud quietly backs it up and keeps your devices in step.

  • No Routey server — your data syncs through your private iCloud database.
  • No account to create. No tracking, no analytics SDKs.
  • A quiet sync status keeps you informed without ever blocking the screen.

Full details are in the Privacy Policy.

In the truck (CarPlay)

V1.2
